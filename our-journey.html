From Farm to Grill

Our Journey

How JA-certified Wagyu from the farms of Kagoshima and Iwate found its home in Royal Oak, Auckland — and why that journey matters.

和牛

The Conviction

Not All Wagyu
Is Equal.

WAGYU SHOGUN began with a simple observation: "Wagyu" had become a word stretched far beyond its meaning. Across New Zealand, beef labelled Wagyu bore little resemblance to what Japan's most dedicated farms were producing — and the gap was enormous.

We set out not to approximate authentic Japanese Wagyu, but to bring the genuine article — sourced exactly as it should be, handled with the same care it receives at origin.

農協

The Source

JA-Certified.
ZEN-NOH Direct.

Our Kagoshima Kuroge Wagyu is sourced through ZEN-NOH — the national agricultural cooperative of Japan (JA Group) — guaranteeing certified A5 grade on every cut, directly from Kagoshima Prefecture's most trusted Kuroge Washu farms.

This isn't branding. JA certification is Japan's most rigorous agricultural standard. A5 on the 15-point BMS scale means extraordinary intramuscular fat, exceptional tenderness, and a flavour that cannot be replicated in any other beef on earth.

鹿児島

Kagoshima

Japan's Premier
Wagyu Prefecture.

Kagoshima Prefecture, on the southern island of Kyushu, has consistently produced Japan's most decorated Wagyu cattle. The warm climate, clean water, and generations of farming knowledge combine to create Kuroge Washu — Black Hair Wagyu — of unmatched consistency and quality.

When Kagoshima Kuroge Wagyu wins national competitions year after year, it is not an accident. It is the result of farmers who treat their work as a lifelong craft. We honour that work by serving it without compromise.

岩手

Iwate Iwachiku

A Different
Character.

Alongside Kagoshima, we selected Iwate Iwachiku Wagyu from the cooler northern reaches of Honshu. A multiple award-winning Wagyu with a deeper, richer personality — bolder umami, a different fat structure, a genuinely distinct experience.

Our dual-selection set exists for exactly this reason: to let guests experience both in a single sitting, and understand that even within the finest tier of Japanese Wagyu, there are real, meaningful differences worth tasting.

焼肉

The Experience

Self-style Yakiniku —
The Right Way.

We serve authentic self-style Yakiniku — you grill your own Wagyu at the table, to your own preferred doneness. There is no performance here, no theatre. Just you, the grill, and one of the world's finest ingredients.

We deliberately cap sessions at 15–20 portions. Not for exclusivity's sake, but because quality attention — to the sourcing, the preparation, the service — cannot be scaled beyond what we can do properly. This is a boutique experience by design, and that is how it will remain.

将軍

Royal Oak, Auckland

From Everyday NZ
to the World.

726 Manukau Road is more than an address. It is where Japan's most certified Wagyu farming tradition meets the warmth and directness of New Zealand hospitality.

Our mission is unchanged from the day we opened: to bring authentic Japanese Wagyu — not an approximation of it — to everyday New Zealand. And from here, to the world. This is our journey. We hope you're part of it.
